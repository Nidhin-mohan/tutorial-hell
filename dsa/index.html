The rules (read once, then obey)

The floor

  • 3 sessions per week. Any day, any time. Total freedom inside the week, zero freedom about the week.
  • Week runs Monday → Sunday. 3/3 = week closed. Under 3 = tracker shows it. No hiding.
  • Two weeks in a row under 2 sessions → drop the floor to 2/week or pause properly. No zombie-dragging.

Session structure (40 min, every time) — attempt FIRST

  • 2 min — recap where you are (look at this page). Pick the next untouched problem.
  • 20 min — attempt it solo, cold, before watching the lesson or reading the pattern note. Narrate your reasoning out loud. Not solving it is the expected outcome on a new pattern, not a failure — the struggle is what makes the explanation stick after.
  • 10 min — now open the phase's lesson / pattern note (whichever exists) and watch/read only what's relevant to this one problem.
  • 8 min — check solution, verify time/space complexity, mark status honestly.

Why the order flipped (2026-07-19): lecture-first was making sessions boring and passive — attempt-first makes the problem yours before the video answers it for you.

Status marks (be honest — lying only wastes your own weeks)

  • ✅ Solo — solved with no hints, no peeking. Only these count toward progress.
  • 🟡 Hints — needed a nudge. Goes to revision queue.
  • ❌ Solution — needed the full answer. Goes to revision queue. Re-attempt from scratch after 3+ days.
  • A phase only closes when every problem is ✅. Repeat problems until they are. Slow is fine, fake isn't.

The lazy-person protocol

  • The 5-minute rule: your only commitment is to open the problem. Starting is the whole battle.
  • Dead-energy day? Do one revision-queue problem in 15 min. It counts as a session. Something > nothing.
  • Missed a session? Skip it. Never double up next day — that's the burnout path.
  • 1 rest day minimum per week. Mandatory. It's part of the plan, not a break from it.

Anti-AI-crutch rule

  • Solve first, alone, in a plain editor. Online assessments in 2026 are proctored with anti-cheat — you must be able to do this bare-hands.
  • After marking status, you MAY ask an AI to review your solution like a senior would. Never before.
